Overview

Kick-start your future in the accountancy profession with a professionally accredited degree. 100% of our Accountancy students are employed or in further study within six months of graduating.

The Bachelor of Accountancy (BAcc) course is designed to help propel your career in accountancy or the wider financial sector.

The programme allows you to gain significant examination exemptions from professional bodies, meaning you can progress quickly with external qualifications after graduation.


Duration
Four-Year Honours or Three-Year Honours.

How will you be taught?

A combination of lectures, seminars, practical workshops and tutorials. There are small group tutorials throughout each semester, providing feedback and reinforcement of lecture topics.

How Will You Be Assessed?

A combination of coursework and examinations.

If you’re an Honours student, all advanced modules undertaken during the final two years count equally towards your overall degree classification.

The four-year Honours Degree includes writing a dissertation on an accounting topic.

A Degree of Flexibility

Flexibility is built into the curriculum at Stirling. In Year 1 and the first part of Year 2, you will generally study a range of subjects across a variety of disciplines - which will naturally enhance your main degree subject, or allow you to try something completely new. Midway through your second year, we ask you to choose your graduating subject(s). You have the chance to stick with your original subject choice, add a second subject for a Combined Honours, or change path completely. The choice is yours.

If you’re not studying full time, individual modules can be taken from this degree and added to over time. This gives you greater flexibility to do the degree at your own pace.

Rewarding Talent

We offer the chance to be part of a University that values the drive and focus of our students, and a number of accounting and finance prizes are offered.

ICAS Charles Scott Prize:
A prize of £100 is awarded annually to the Bachelor of Accountancy student with the highest overall performance in modules ACCU9A1 and ACCU9A2 in the same academic session.

KPMG Prize:
There are three prizes of £200 awarded annually to the students with the highest overall performance in each of the modules ACCU9A4, ACCU9AA and ACCU9A6.

Accounting and Finance Undergraduate Dissertation Prize:
A research-based prize of £200 is open to all students on the single Honours BAcc course and is awarded to the student(s) who submits the best undergraduate dissertation(s). The prize may be shared if more than one dissertation achieves the highest grade.

Ede and Ravenscroft Prize:

The Ede & Ravenscroft prize of £700 is awarded on academic merit to the top graded 2nd Year student within each School.

What employers say

Quick Facts

Our Accountancy graduates currently work in over 21 countries and for some of the world’s leading financial bodies.

Career Opportunities

Accountancy offers a wide choice of careers with organisations in every field, from healthcare and sport, to charities and national governments. Your professionally accredited degree will help you to pursue your career in the direction you want to take. Careers include financial and management accounting, auditing, taxation, consultancy and financial services.

Coming from an accountancy background, employers will see that you possess a strong business-focused education and our graduates have a fantastic employment record, going on to work at leading and global businesses.

Recent graduates have kick-started their career working in the following roles, for example:

  • Associate, PWC
  • Commercial and Performance Analyst, SSE
  • Audit Associate, Deloitte UK
  • Graduate Finance Analyst, Aston Martin

Contributing to bottom-line performance and being at the forefront of the industry, past graduates have gone on to become established leaders, for example: ICAS Investment Director; J.P. Morgan Head of Quality Review; Energy

Australia Finance Director; and Chief Financial Officers. Find out about our Alumni mentoring programme, open to all Year 3 and Year 4 students across the Management School.

Enhance Your Business Skills

At Stirling you will discover and develop new skills that are sought by employers and will get you ready for the workplace – so you can make a difference in the global marketplace. At every stage of the course you will have opportunities to develop work-related, transferable skills including using industry software such as Sage programs and Microsoft Excel.

Our world-class teaching follows through to tutorials, where we encourage active tutorial participation and group presentations. Your communication and interpersonal skills are needed by employers in all areas of business.

You will learn to question and delve deeper into topics, developing your critical thinking and research skills. Embracing your love for numbers, the Accountancy course will enhance your problem solving, numeracy, and analytical and logical thinking.
